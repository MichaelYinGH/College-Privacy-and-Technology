Guide to Harvard Surveillance Cameras

A textual and visual guide to Harvard's surveillance camera policies and placement

726

The number of surveillance cameras counted in and around Harvard University

The region comprised Harvard Yard, Harvard Business School, and Harvard Stadium

The counting was conducted in Fall 2021 through Professor Jim Waldo's CS 105: Privacy and Technology

The 726 figure reflects one group's count; some groups spotted fewer cameras, while others counted more

There's actually no good way to know exactly how many surveillance cameras there are!

Of the 726 cameras found:

  • 146 were around Harvard Yard and the river,
  • 40 around the athletics facilities,
  • 409 in and around Harvard Business School, and
  • 131 in and around the new Science and Engineering Complex

Science Center Plaza Cam 24/7 Livestream

Some cameras are maintained by local stores or used solely for security purposes

Others are much more public, however; for instance, Harvard's Common Spaces YouTube channel maintains a 24/7 livestream of the Science Center Plaza

Types of Cameras

While some of Harvard's cameras are stereotypically camera-looking, there are many different types of cameras that come in all shapes and sizes

For instance, this is a picture of a flat eyeball-esque camera in the Harvard athletics facilities

Harvard's policies on the installation and use of video cameras (which can be found here) include:
  • Each School or unit at Harvard has at least one person who reviews and approves requests to install video cameras and check their data
  • Cameras for security and facilities management purposes can't be installed in dorm rooms, restrooms, locker rooms, classrooms, or offices
  • Beyond these University-wide rules, Schools/units can create further restrictions on camera placements
  • Exceptions can be made to these rules if there are threats to safety or property
  • Unless requested by law enforcement, video cameras cannot record audio or be deliberately concealed; specific notices of camera presence are not required, though
  • Camera data can be used for investigations (criminal or legal), crime prevention, maintenance, construction monitoring, or any other "legitimate institutional purpose"
