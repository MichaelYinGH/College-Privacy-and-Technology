iPhone Privacy Guide

An easy-to-follow visual guide showing you exactly how to change your iPhone privacy settings

Security & Authentication Settings

Turn on 2 factor authentication for unfamiliar devices

Settings → [Your name] → Password & Security → Turn on two-factor authentication

Ensure that your Face ID can’t be used when you’re sleeping or have your eyes closed

Settings > Face ID & Passcode → Require Attention for Face ID

Change what someone is able to do from your lock screen when your phone is locked

Settings > Face ID & Passcode → Allow Access When Locked → consider disabling: Reply with Message, Return Missed Calls, Notification Center

Change what previews are visible on your lock screen when your phone is locked

Settings > Notifications > Show Previews → When Unlocked / Never

Specific Apps: Notifications > [App name] > Show Previews.

Privacy Settings

Update app permissions: Settings > Privacy

  • (1) Location Services: You can easily scroll through your apps to see which apps have access to your location and you can click on them to modify their access, disable their access from your “Precise Location,” and see their explanation for why they need your location.
  • (2) Tracking: This setting controls whether or not apps can track your activity across other apps and websites. We recommend disabling “Allow Apps to Request to Track” which automatically disables all requests and best protects your privacy.
  • (3) Photos: This setting controls what photos each of your apps has access to. We recommend limiting your apps access to your photos to “Selected Photos.”
  • You can also control the access apps have to your other information, notably your (4) Contacts, (5) Camera, and (6) Health data.

Siri Settings: Settings → Siri & Search

We recommend: disabling “Listen for ‘Hey Siri’”

Enable automatic updates to your OS and apps:

Enabling automatic updates allows app and iOS developers to add security patches without delay to your device.
iOS Updates: Settings > General > Software Update
App Updates: Settings > App Store → App Updates
